About Parkinson's Disease Detection System

Project Overview

This system uses advanced machine learning techniques to enable early detection of Parkinson's Disease through multimodal data analysis using ONLY real patient datasets.

Key Principle: This system uses NO synthetic data. All features come from real, publicly available medical research datasets.

Parkinson's Disease (PD) is the second most common brain disorder, affecting millions worldwide. Early detection is crucial as it allows for timely intervention and significantly improves patient quality of life. Traditional diagnosis methods rely on clinical observation, which can be subjective and may miss early-stage symptoms.

Baseline Model:

Primary Model:

  • Kernel types: Linear, RBF, Polynomial, Sigmoid

About Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's Disease is a long-term brain disorder that mainly affects body movement. It is a neurodegenerative disease, which means it slowly damages brain cells over time.

Common Symptoms:
  • Tremor: Shaking that usually starts in the hands or fingers
  • Bradykinesia: Slowed movement making simple tasks difficult
  • Muscle Rigidity: Stiffness that can occur in any part of the body
  • Postural Instability: Balance problems that can lead to falls
  • Speech Changes: Softer voice, slurred speech, or monotone speaking
  • Writing Changes: Handwriting may become smaller (micrographia)
Why Early Detection Matters:

Early detection allows for timely treatment, which can slow disease progression, manage symptoms more effectively, and significantly improve quality of life. With early intervention, patients can maintain independence and functionality for longer periods.
